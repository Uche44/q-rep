Yes. In fact, that's probably the strongest choice because it aligns with the QIE ecosystem branding while still preserving the trust and professionalism that a reputation platform needs.

### Core Palette

**Background**

```css
#0A0A0A
```

or

```css
#111111
```

A deep black, not pure `#000000`. Pure black can feel harsh and creates too much contrast.

---

**Primary Text**

```css
#FFFFFF
```

For headings, reputation scores, names, and important metrics.

---

**Secondary Text**

```css
#A1A1AA
```

For descriptions, timestamps, and metadata.

---

**QIE Accent Pink**

```css
#FF4FA3
```

or whatever the official QIE pink is.

Use it for:

* Reputation score highlights
* Active tabs
* Progress indicators
* Charts
* Selected states
* Important CTAs

---

## What You Should Avoid

Don't make everything pink.

Bad:

```text
Pink cards
Pink buttons
Pink text
Pink borders
Pink charts
Pink badges
```

The platform starts looking like a crypto meme app.

Instead:

### 80% Black/White

### 15% Gray

### 5% Pink

The pink becomes premium because it's scarce.

## Glassmorphism?

If you're using pink and black already, I'd keep glass effects very subtle.

Think:

```css
background: rgba(255,255,255,0.04);
backdrop-filter: blur(12px);
border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.08);
```

not

```css
background: rgba(255,79,163,0.4);
blur: 50px;
```

The second approach quickly becomes "crypto dashboard 2022."

### My recommendation

**Theme:** Dark Minimalism

* Background: `#0A0A0A`
* Surfaces: `#141414`
* Cards: `#1A1A1A`
* Primary Text: `#FFFFFF`
* Secondary Text: `#A1A1AA`
* Accent: QIE Pink (`#FF4FA3` or official equivalent)

For a reputation product, that combination will look modern, ecosystem-aligned, and trustworthy without sacrificing readability or professionalism.
